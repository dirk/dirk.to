21 May 2015 - 29 May 2015

On the twenty-first of May, 2015, I began a road trip with my friend Ryan Gonzalez across the United States of America from Charleston, South Carolina to San Francisco, California. This is the semi-live diary of that voyage.

21 May 2015

Hello goodbye

A mildly tearful goodbye to the parents; a happy welcome from Ryan.

A last sight of home. So long, Charleston; it's been fun.

Obligatory indulgent grille shot.

Stealing himself away from work to say hi.

Don't think I'm going to especially miss the endless pine forests and weird Carolinian suburbs.

22 May 2015

The first leg

We set off some time around 7 or 8 in the morning from Ryan's parents' in Greenwood, South Carolina, or as I would refer to it with characteristic Charlestonian fashion, "Yet Another City In South Carolina That I've Never Heard Of". There's a reason why the rest of the state doesn't like people from Charleston.

I figure out the seat memory functions before we departed. (Photo by Ryan)

Ryan getting familiar with all the various gagetry on the 335i.

Weird old JC Penney store we encountered during (sadly) the first Starbucks stop of the day.

Ryan was not as excited about the building.

Picturesque little South Carolina town we passed through on the way out of the state. I forgot its name.

Beautiful "god rays" over some river somewhere in Mississippi. Or at least I think it was Mississippi. It, Alabama, and Louisiana all sort of blended in together.

23 May 2015

Into Texas

Today we got a first taste of the best thing about Texas and the worst thing about Texas. The best thing is that Texas has figured out that it's perfectly fine to have 80 miles-per-hour speed limits. If only the rest of this country would realize that. Despite the lovely speed limit, we also discovered first-hand that Texas is big, really big.

In Austin Ryan and I split ways to meet up with respective old friends. I got a good taste of the special (ie. annoying) character of Austin: endless traffic and long lines everywhere.

Stopped for gas a bit of the way into Texas; turned off of the interstate on the way to Austin.

Visited a meadery in Austin. Discovered I like mead.

24 May 2015

Texas from dawn to dusk

We spent the entire day in Texas today on the long road from Austin to El Paso. It was very long, very boring, very full of desert.

The morning was still damp from the night's rain. We stopped for gas and a snack at a crossing named Junction, Texas.

Quite a bug collection had been amassed, and yet more were to be collected.

We drove through a great deal of desert on the way to El Paso.

After meeting up with my cousin and their spouse, we all headed up to a nearby mountain (literally a mile away) to get a beautiful view of El Paso and Ju√°rez.

A weirdly-quick drive down through the city brought us to the USA-Mexico border fence: the great metal testament to the stupidity of the human race.

We found life in a really goddamn depressing place.

A farcical boondoggle and affront to common decency & kindness.

25 May 2015

A whole lot of desert

Leaving El Paso we took a detour to visit White Sands National Monument, the largest gypsum salt dunes in the world. (It even smelled like the beach!) We then headed across New Mexico—winding along back-road highways—and into Arizona towards Phoenix.

Ryan doing his action-movie hero walk down into the valley.

Down in the valleys between the dunes there is an eerie silence. The salt sand seems to absorb and block all sound from the other side, leaving just the slight rustle of wind and one's own movement & breathing.

From White Sands we headed across New Mexico towards Arizona. Straddling the border was a remote . We wound through the twisty, exhilarating mountain roads; after what felt like half an hour of roaring through the passes we arrived—hearts pounding—at a breathtaking overlook.

Cliffs loomed around us; and ahead the view opened up to reveal a landscape of smaller mountains, rolling hills, and vast plains.

26 May 2015

Sands into Southern California

From Phoenix we drove to Los Angeles; the landscape gradually changed from desert scrub to the sand and greens of Southern California.

Along the way we ran into an expanse of massive sand dunes. The color was otherworldly: a bright rust with hints of cream.

Of course I also snagged a picture of the car.

27 May 2015

Up the Pacific coast

We set off from Los Angeles up the Pacific Coast Highway. The slightly confusing route and my navigation goofs combined to put us a bit behind schedule, but exuberant driving along the windy cliffside roads made that time back handily.

The fields of grass and scrub along the cliffside were lit in a neat blue haze from the sun and the sea.

The rocks and greenery had an incredible vibrancy in the light.

Pulled off from the road for pictures.

We arrived at Carmel-by-the-Sea in the early evening. Thankfully there was enough light left to walk down the hill to the beach. The sand there was surprisingly white, and the overcast skies gave a cool leaden light to the shore.

28 May 2015

A visit to Lake Tahoe

We had one last day left to spend, so we drove from Carmel up to Lake Tahoe. The legends of its beauty turned out to be 100% true.

A small park by the lake afforded views of the surrounding mountains and the forests &s; fields around the shore.

Ryan forging ahead among the rocks.

We then headed up into the mountains and hiked along a short trail up to some rock formations and a pretty little glen between two low peaks.

29 May 2015

Home!

We made it to our nice little home on Potrero Hill.
